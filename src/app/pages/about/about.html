Our Legacy

PUMA is one of the world’s leading sports brands, designing, developing, selling, and marketing footwear, apparel, and accessories. For over 75 years, PUMA has relentlessly pushed sports and culture forward by creating fast products for the world’s fastest athletes.

Founded in 1948 by Rudolf Dassler, PUMA has a rich history of innovation and excellence in performance. From track fields to football stadiums, basketball courts to urban streets, PUMA’s influence spans across sport and lifestyle alike.

Our mission is to be the Fastest Sports Brand in the world. We are constantly innovating and evolving — not only in design and technology but also in sustainability and social responsibility. Whether you're an athlete chasing your best time, or a fan living the culture, PUMA empowers you to move forward with speed and style.

1954

In 1954, the first world record is held by a pair of PUMA running shoes. Heinz Fütterer matches the 100m world record in Yokohama, Japan with an amazing time of 10.2 seconds. In true PUMA Athlete fashion, he adds to the success four years later when he sets the world record in the 4x100m relay. This is a huge achievement for the young company.

The Elemental Design

In 1958, the second brand logo is patented: the FORMSTRIP Originally created to stabilise the foot inside the shoe, it is now the PUMA trademark found on almost all of PUMA's footwear. It is also used as a design element on apparel products.And it was just in time too - thanks to emerging media, the FORMSTRIP became visible all over the world!
